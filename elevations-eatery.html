What Makes Elevations Special

Elevations Eatery and Bar sits right on downtown Elkhorn Avenue, making it one of the most convenient dining spots for visitors exploring Estes Park's main shopping district. The restaurant operates as a casual bar and grill rather than fine dining, but reviewers consistently praise the food quality that exceeds expectations for the laid-back atmosphere. With 563+ Google reviews averaging 4.4 stars, the restaurant delivers consistently good meals at reasonable prices.

The elk stew appears in 28 reviews as the standout signature dish, with multiple reviewers calling it "mind-blowing" and "amazing." Wild game options extend to bison burgers and elk burgers, sourcing local Colorado ingredients. The kitchen handles special dietary needs well, with clearly labeled vegetarian dishes and gluten-free options including gluten-free burger buns.

Service receives frequent praise across reviews, with servers named Ksusha, Brad, Carlos, Nayen, Ivy, and Marija specifically mentioned for excellent service. The restaurant stays open later than many Estes Park spots, with 8pm closing making it valuable for travelers arriving after peak dinner hours when other restaurants have closed.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Elk Stew - This signature dish dominates positive reviews with 28 mentions. Reviewers describe it as "hearty," "mind-blowing," and worth ordering every visit. The stew features tender elk meat in a rich broth, perfect after a day hiking in Rocky Mountain National Park. One reviewer noted it was "some of the very best" they'd tasted.

Bison Burger ($18) - The Classic Bison Burger comes with mushrooms, Swiss cheese, and caramelized onions on a brioche bun. Reviews mention it as juicy and flavorful, with 23 mentions making it one of the most-ordered items. The burger comes with lettuce, tomato, onions, and pickles on the side, and you can add fries, garden salad, or fruit for $3.

Santa Fe Chicken Burger ($17) - Multiple reviews specifically praise the house-made pepper jack and pepper hash cheese sauce that comes with this burger. One reviewer called the sauce "AMAZING" and wished they could buy it to take home. The sauce also works perfectly as a dip for the restaurant's highly-praised french fries.

Rainbow Trout - Fresh trout appears in 10 reviews as consistently good. The restaurant sources local fish when possible, and reviewers note the trout arrives cooked properly with good seasoning and sides. One couple specifically returned for the trout after trying other menu items on previous visits.

üí° Pro Tip: Reviewers consistently rave about the french fries at Elevations. Multiple reviews mention "the fries are fantastic" and "the fries are great." Order them as your side ($7) or with a burger for $3 extra - they're crispy, well-seasoned, and perfect with that pepper hash cheese sauce.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Elevations excels for post-hiking meals when you're hungry after exploring Rocky Mountain National Park and want downtown convenience. The casual bar atmosphere works well for travelers who want quality food without dressing up or dealing with formal service. Late diners appreciate the 8pm closing when other Estes Park restaurants have already shut down. Vegetarians and vegans find clearly labeled options including the Mediterranean Bowl and Roasted Squash Bowl. The location makes it perfect for combining dinner with downtown shopping or catching a movie at the nearby Historic Park Theater. Looking for more dining options? Check out Bird & Jim Restaurant for wild game, The Hive Brewery for craft beer, or Ember Restaurant for lake views.

Not ideal for: Fine dining seekers will find the atmosphere too casual - one reviewer expected upscale dining from photos but found a bar and grill instead (though they were pleasantly surprised by food quality). The downtown Elkhorn Avenue location creates parking challenges, with reviewers mentioning "somewhat difficult to find parking." Budget diners ordering appetizers may find portions small - one reviewer called the $20 cheese plate "underwhelming" with brown apple slices. The decor gets mixed reviews with one visitor calling it "lackluster and uninspired" despite praising the food.

The restaurant handles groups well with suitable seating for all group sizes mentioned in reviews. Wheelchair accessibility appears available though not extensively detailed in reviews. The moderate to quiet noise level allows easy conversation. Service quality stays consistent with prompt, friendly staff handling special requests and dietary restrictions without issues.

Practical Information

Reservations: The restaurant offers online reservations through their website (elevationseatery.com) and third-party booking sites. Reviewers mention making reservations "just to be safe" and getting seated immediately even when tables remained open. For downtown convenience and peace of mind during peak summer and weekend dinner hours, book ahead. Walk-ins work but may face waits during prime 6-7:30pm dining times.

Parking strategy: Street parking on Elkhorn Avenue runs paid during business hours, then switches to free after 5pm. Multiple reviews mention parking difficulty, with one noting "somewhat difficult to find parking." A larger free parking lot sits within walking distance. Arrive early for lunch or plan to walk a few blocks from peripheral parking areas during peak times.

Best times to visit: Lunch (11am-2pm) typically has better parking availability and shorter waits. The kitchen maintains the same menu and pricing throughout the day, so lunch offers good value without dinner crowds. Late dining works well here since they stay open until 8pm when many Estes Park restaurants close earlier. One local reviewer specifically praised the late hours as "a wise thing in a tourist town."

Special features: The craft cocktail menu receives consistent praise, with signature drinks like the Smoked Oaxacan (served in an oak smoking box), Smoked Old Fashioned, Lilikoi Spritz, and Lemon Drop Martini mentioned positively. The extensive spirits selection includes bourbon, whiskey, and scotch options. Bartenders Carlos and Nayen get named specifically for excellent craft cocktails. The restaurant also offers 2-for-1 mimosas that draw brunch crowds.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does dinner cost at Elevations?

Expect $20-30 per person for most meals. Burgers run $17-19, salads and bowls $17-24. Add $3 for fries or salad sides. Appetizers and cocktails increase the total. One reviewer noted spending $50-100 per person when adding drinks and appetizers like the ahi tartare. Most reviews mention the pricing as reasonable for the quality delivered.

Do I need reservations?

Reservations help but aren't always required. One reviewer made an online reservation and got seated right away even with open tables visible. For dinner Friday-Saturday or peak summer weekends, book through their website or call (970) 586-6900. Lunch typically accommodates walk-ins more easily.

Where do I park?

Downtown Elkhorn Avenue parking runs paid during business hours, free after 5pm. Multiple reviews mention parking difficulty, so arrive early or walk from nearby lots. One reviewer notes "a big free parking lot not too far on foot." Street parking exists but fills quickly during peak tourist season.

What is Elevations known for?

The elk stew dominates reviews with 28 mentions, consistently called "mind-blowing" and "amazing." Wild game options including bison burgers (23 mentions) and elk burgers (20 mentions) define the menu. The restaurant also earns praise for staying open late (until 8pm) when other Estes Park spots close earlier, and for excellent craft cocktails made by skilled bartenders.

Is the atmosphere casual or fine dining?

Definitively casual. One reviewer expected fine dining from photos but found a bar and grill atmosphere (though they were pleasantly surprised by food quality). Reviews describe "moderate noise" and a relaxed vibe. One reviewer called the decor "lackluster and uninspired," but most focus on praising the food and service rather than ambiance.

What are the vegetarian/vegan options?

Multiple clearly labeled vegetarian and vegan options exist. The Mediterranean Bowl and Roasted Squash Bowl both come vegan and gluten-free. The Blackberry Salad and Fennel & Orange Salad offer vegan options. One reviewer notes "clearly labeled vegetarian dishes" and recommends the restaurant highly for vegetarians. The kitchen accommodates special dietary requests.

Do they have gluten-free options?

Yes. Multiple reviews mention gluten-free burger buns available for $2 extra. Several bowls and salads come naturally gluten-free and are labeled (GF) on the menu. One reviewer specifically noted his wife could enjoy herself with the gluten-free burger option.

How fast is the service?

Reviews consistently mention fast service. One reviewer noted "we got our food super fast," while another said "food came out so fast!" Service quality receives 5-star ratings in most reviews, with servers described as friendly, efficient, attentive, and prompt. The bartenders also earn specific praise for quick, skilled cocktail preparation.

Is it good for families with kids?

Yes. The restaurant offers a kids menu ($12-13) with chicken tenders, mac & cheese, grilled cheese, and butter-parmesan noodles. The casual atmosphere makes families comfortable. One reviewer noted "it's just a big empty store front so the kids can run around," suggesting a relaxed environment for families with active children.

What are the best cocktails to try?

The Smoked Oaxacan arrives in an oak smoking box for dramatic presentation. Reviewers also praise the Smoked Old Fashioned, Lemon Drop Martini (described as "tart, cold, and exactly what the moment called for"), Lilikoi Spritz, and Tropical Sour. Bartenders Carlos and Nayen get named specifically for excellent craft cocktails. The 2-for-1 mimosas draw brunch crowds.
