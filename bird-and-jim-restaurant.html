Bird & Jim Review: Menu, Prices & Worth It?

Complete Guide to Estes Park's Mountain Restaurant

Bird & Jim brings rustic mountain dining to Estes Park with locally-sourced wild game, hearty American fare, and a warm cabin atmosphere. This casual eatery serves elk, bison, and their famous smoked pheasant chowder alongside classic burgers and salads. Expect $30-50 per person for dinner with drinks. Located steps from Rocky Mountain National Park, Bird & Jim attracts hikers refueling after trail time and locals seeking quality mountain cuisine in a laid-back setting.

What Makes Bird & Jim Special

Bird & Jim stands out in Estes Park for serving locally-sourced wild game you won't find at typical mountain restaurants. They work directly with Colorado ranchers and hunters to bring elk, bison, and wild pheasant to your plate. The restaurant occupies a renovated space in the Alpine Trail Ridge Inn with high ceilings, wood accents, and mismatched cloth napkins that create genuine mountain charm without feeling contrived.

Their open kitchen concept lets you watch chefs prepare your meal while you eat. The space mixes cozy couches with regular dining tables and high-top bar seating, giving you options whether you're settling in for a long dinner or grabbing a quick bite at the bar.

Over 3,100 Google reviews averaging 4.5 stars speak to consistent quality. Diners repeatedly mention the warm service, generous portions, and unique menu items that showcase Colorado ingredients in ways that feel authentic rather than touristy.

üí° Pro Tip: Arrive 30 minutes before your reservation and grab a drink at the bar while you wait. The bartenders are friendly, local craft beers are on tap, and you'll avoid standing in the waiting area during busy dinner service.

The Dining Experience

Walking into Bird & Jim feels like entering a well-loved mountain cabin. The huge space features high ceilings with exposed beams, woody mountain decor, and local art for sale on the walls. Natural light floods through large windows during lunch, while warm lighting creates intimate ambiance at dinner.

Service strikes the right balance between attentive and relaxed. Staff know the menu well and can explain the difference between elk striploin and elk tenderloin or suggest wine pairings. During peak hours, you might experience slower service as they prioritize getting food right over rushing tables. Noise level stays moderate even when packed, allowing easy conversation without shouting.

The crowd shifts throughout the day. Lunch brings hikers fresh off trails from Rocky Mountain National Park, still wearing dusty boots and backpacks. Dinner attracts more locals, couples on vacation, and families celebrating special occasions. Weekend brunch (Saturday-Sunday 10:30am-8pm) draws a mix of both.

Menu Highlights & Pricing

Bird & Jim's menu focuses on hearty American fare with Colorado wild game as the star. Appetizers run $12-24, with house-made potato chips and onion dip at the lower end and tuna poke at the top. The middle section includes soups (famous smoked pheasant chowder), salads, and sandwiches ranging $14-22. Entrees span $24-52, with standard burgers around $20 and specialty wild game dishes hitting the upper range.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Smoked Pheasant Chowder ($9 cup / $14 bowl) - This appears in more reviews than any other dish. The chowder features tender pheasant meat in a creamy base with vegetables, served with soft, warm cornbread rounds. Customers call it "comforting," "the best soup ever," and order it on every visit. Order a bowl as your meal or get a cup to start if you're trying wild game entrees.

Buffalo Burger with Tomato-Bacon Jam ($20) - Leaner than beef but juicy from proper cooking, this burger combines buffalo meat with sweet-savory jam that cuts the richness. Comes with fries unless you upgrade to sweet potato waffle fries. Reviewers consistently rate this as the best burger in Estes Park, with some claiming it beats Denver steakhouse burgers.

The Carnivore Plate ($52) - If you're trying wild game for the first time, this sampler delivers. You get elk striploin, filet mignon, cheddar game sausage (buffalo-jalapeno-cheddar), and broccolini on one plate. Portions let two people share if you order apps, or satisfy one hungry hiker. The elk striploin consistently gets praised as "the best elk I've ever had."

Lamb Rack ($44) - Cooked medium-rare with roasted tomatoes that taste marinated before roasting. Multiple reviewers call this "the best lamb chops ever" and note the unique flavor of the tomatoes. The preparation shows technical skill without overcomplicating the dish.

üí° Pro Tip: If you're unsure about wild game, order the house chips and onion dip ($12) first. These thick-cut, crispy chips appear in dozens of reviews as surprisingly good. Use them to gauge your hunger level before committing to a $40+ entree. You can always order more food, but you can't un-order an expensive dish you won't finish.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Bird & Jim excels for post-hike refueling, especially after a long day in Rocky Mountain National Park. Families appreciate the kids' menu, relaxed dress code, and mix of adventurous wild game plus familiar chicken fingers. Couples seeking a nice meal without formal dress codes or pretentious service find the sweet spot here. Large groups (4-8 people) can spread out on the couches or book the outdoor patio area. Looking for more dining options? Check out Estes Park restaurants with views or Antlers Rooftop Tavern.

Not ideal for: Budget diners need to know that $30-50 per person adds up quickly for groups. Quick meals don't happen here during peak hours when kitchen gets backed up - some reviews mention 60+ minute waits for entrees on busy nights. Vegetarians and vegans have options (the Hallet's Peak vegan bowl gets praise) but the menu clearly targets meat-eaters. If you want quiet, romantic ambiance, the moderate noise level and casual vibe might feel too relaxed.

The restaurant handles dietary restrictions well with gluten-free options marked on the menu and kitchen willing to accommodate requests. Wheelchair accessibility works with a ramp at entrance. Service dogs are welcome. The mix of couch seating, regular tables, and bar spots means you can find your preferred setting.

Complete Practical Guide

Parking is straightforward with a free lot directly at Bird & Jim. During peak dinner hours (6-7:30pm), this lot fills up but you can park at the Bird's Nest lot across Moraine Avenue - literally 50 steps away. Street parking exists on Moraine but the free lots make that unnecessary. No parking meters, no validation needed, no stress.

Reservations through Google/Resy or calling (970) 586-9832 become essential for dinner Friday-Saturday and holiday weekends. The restaurant welcomes walk-ins but you might wait 30-60 minutes during prime time or get stuck with couch seating when you wanted a proper table. Lunch reservations matter less except peak summer months. One reviewer called ahead Monday night, was told no wait, arrived 10 minutes later to find dining room full - so reserve even when they say it's not necessary.

Money-Saving Tips & Hacks

Happy hour (open to 4pm) drops prices on select appetizers and drinks. Multiple reviews mention this draws crowds, suggesting significant savings. Call to ask which items get discounted - the restaurant's policy might change seasonally but consistently runs until 4pm daily.

Lunch pricing appears similar to dinner on most items, but ordering strategically matters. Build a meal from the appetizer and sides menu (chips + chowder + side salad = $25 total) instead of buying a $20 burger. Share one wild game entree between two people if you've hiked all day and add sides rather than ordering two full entrees.

Skip the add-ons unless you're truly hungry. Burgers come with fries already - the $4 upgrade to sweet potato waffle fries tastes good according to reviews but isn't necessary. The $10 mac and cheese side gets mixed reviews, with several calling it "bland" and "the worst mac and cheese ever," so save that $10.

üí° Money Hack: Order the Smoked Pheasant Chowder bowl ($14), cornbread (included), and house chips with onion dip ($12) for a $26 meal that will completely fill you up. You get to try their most famous dish, experience the wild game they're known for, and spend half what you'd pay for an elk entree. This combo appears in multiple five-star reviews from budget-conscious diners.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does dinner cost at Bird & Jim?

Expect $30-50 per person for dinner including appetizer, entree, and one drink. A burger with fries and a beer runs about $28. Wild game entrees like elk or the Carnivore plate push you toward $50-65 with drinks. Couples typically spend $80-120 total for full dinner with apps and dessert.

Do I need reservations at Bird & Jim?

Yes for dinner, especially Friday-Saturday and summer weekends. Book through Google/Resy or call (970) 586-9832 at least 1-2 weeks ahead for prime dining times. Walk-ins work for lunch most days and early/late dinner (5pm or 8:30pm+) but expect 30-60 minute waits during peak hours without reservation.

Where do I park for Bird & Jim?

Use the free parking lot directly at the restaurant on Moraine Avenue. If full, park at Bird's Nest lot across the street - it's free and about 50 steps away. Both lots handle the dinner rush well. Street parking exists as backup but the free lots make that unnecessary.

What is Bird & Jim known for?

The Smoked Pheasant Chowder appears in more reviews than any dish - customers call it the best soup they've had and order it every visit. Wild game (elk, bison, buffalo) sourced from Colorado ranchers defines the menu. The casual mountain atmosphere in a renovated cabin-style space draws hikers refueling after Rocky Mountain National Park adventures.

Does Bird & Jim have vegetarian options?

Yes, with vegetarian dishes clearly labeled on the menu. The veggie burger gets praised in reviews as "thoughtfully designed, not just thrown on the menu." Hallet's Peak vegan bowl receives positive mentions. The Arugula Apple Pecan Salad works for vegetarians. However, the menu heavily features meat, so vegans and vegetarians have fewer options than omnivores.

Is Bird & Jim kid-friendly?

Yes, very kid-friendly with a dedicated children's menu including chicken fingers and grilled cheese ($9-10). Reviews mention families with children dining comfortably. The casual dress code means hiking clothes and sneakers work fine. Noise level stays moderate enough that kids won't disturb other diners. High chairs available.

Is Bird & Jim worth the price?

For wild game and quality, yes. You're paying $40-50 for elk or bison you can't easily find elsewhere, sourced locally and prepared well. The $20 burgers match pricing at other Estes Park restaurants but deliver better quality according to reviews. If you want standard American food, cheaper options exist. If you want to experience Colorado wild game in a comfortable setting, the price reflects the product.

When is the best time to go to Bird & Jim?

For smallest crowds: lunch (11am-2pm) Tuesday-Thursday or dinner at 5pm/8pm+ to avoid the 6-7:30pm rush. Happy hour until 4pm offers good value. After hiking Rocky Mountain National Park, most people arrive 5:30-7pm, creating the peak crush. Weekend brunch (Saturday-Sunday starting 10:30am) draws moderate crowds but moves faster than dinner service.

Can I try wild game at Bird & Jim if I've never had it?

Absolutely - start with the Smoked Pheasant Chowder ($14 bowl) to test wild game in a familiar soup format. The Buffalo Burger ($20) tastes similar to beef but leaner. The Carnivore Plate ($52) lets you sample elk, filet mignon, and bison sausage in one order. Staff can explain differences and recommend based on your preferences. Multiple reviews mention first-time wild game eaters loving the elk.

Does Bird & Jim take reservations for large groups?

Yes, call (970) 586-9832 to book for groups of 6 or more. The restaurant has space for large parties with couch areas and outdoor patio options. Book well in advance (2-3 weeks) for summer weekends or holidays. Several reviews mention groups of 5-8 people dining together successfully with reservations.
