What Makes Cousin Pat's Special

Cousin Pat's fills a critical gap in Estes Park dining: it's open late. Most restaurants close by 8 or 9pm, leaving hikers returning from sunset adventures with few options. Pat's stays open until midnight on weekends (11pm weekdays), making it the go-to spot for late-night food and drinks.

The atmosphere delivers classic sports bar vibes - multiple TVs showing games, pool tables, a fireplace in the dining area, and patriotic decor that includes a touching veterans tribute table. Cousin Pat himself is often behind the bar, making margaritas and chatting with guests. When he noticed a couple watching UFC, the waiter turned up the volume. When another guest mentioned being cold after hiking, Pat moved them to a warmer table and fired up a heater.

The menu covers a lot of ground - BBQ, Italian, pub food, pizza. Some dishes shine (fish & chips, truffle mac, cheese curds), while others can miss (inconsistent pizza, sometimes overcooked burgers). It's not destination dining, but it's solid late-night comfort food when you need it.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Fish & Chips ($16.95) - The most-mentioned dish in reviews. Beer-battered Atlantic cod, thick and flaky with thin crispy batter. Comes with fries, coleslaw, and tartar sauce. Among the best fish & chips in Estes Park.

Truffle Mac & Cheese ($16.95) - Creamy, indulgent, with truffle oil that adds depth without overpowering. Pair with a beer for the ultimate comfort meal.

Poutine ($14.95) - Wisconsin cheese curds, fries, and gravy. A Canadian pub classic done right. Great for sharing.

Fried Cheese Curds ($8.95) - Another Wisconsin import. Battered, fried, served with ranch. Order these early as they go fast.

Pat's Margaritas - When Cousin Pat makes them himself, they're excellent. Strong, well-balanced, and worth ordering.

üí° Pro Tip: They serve 32oz draft beers - great value if you're settling in for a game. Pat's a Packers fan, but all teams are welcome.

The Honest Take

At 4.2 stars, Cousin Pat's is solidly good but not exceptional. Food quality varies - the fish & chips and truffle mac deliver consistently, while burgers and pizza can miss (burnt or undercooked). The Italian dishes (alfredo, chicken parm) are hit-or-miss. Service is generally strong and attentive.

The real value here is the atmosphere and hours. Where else can you watch the game, play pool, and eat hot food at 11pm in Estes Park? The patriotic decor and veterans table create a unique, meaningful environment. And if Pat's there, you'll get genuine hospitality from an owner who clearly cares about his guests.

Come for the late hours and pub atmosphere. Stick to the pub classics (fish & chips, poutine, burgers) rather than the Italian dishes. Don't expect fine dining, and you'll have a good time.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Late-night diners (open til midnight), sports fans, post-hike comfort food seekers, groups wanting pool and drinks, anyone looking for food after other restaurants close.

Skip if: You want fine dining, consistency is your priority, you're sensitive to noise (it's a sports bar), or you need quick service during games.

Looking for other options? Check Smokin' Dave's BBQ for better BBQ (but earlier hours) or Ed's Cantina for riverside Mexican.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Cousin Pat's Pub & Grill

What time does Cousin Pat's close?

Cousin Pat's stays open late - until midnight on Friday and Saturday, 11pm Sunday through Thursday. It's one of the few places in Estes Park open past 9pm, making it the go-to for late-night dining after hikes or events.

How much does dinner cost at Cousin Pat's?

Most main dishes run $10-20 per person. Fish & chips, burgers, and pasta dishes are $14-17. Ribs and steaks run higher ($27-30). Appetizers are $8-16. With a drink, expect $20-35 per person.

Is Cousin Pat's good for families?

The restaurant has a family dining area separate from the bar section. Kids menu runs $7.95 with chicken fingers, grilled cheese, mac & cheese, or spaghetti. Booth seating available. It's casual and welcoming for families, especially earlier in the evening.

Can I watch sports at Cousin Pat's?

Cousin Pat's has multiple TVs with all sports packages including NFL, UFC, and more. They'll turn up the volume if you're watching a specific game. Pat is a Packers fan, but all teams are welcome. They sometimes offer touchdown jello shots during games.

Who is Cousin Pat?

Pat is the owner, and he's often there working behind the bar or greeting guests. He's known for making "award-winning" margaritas and for going above and beyond - relocating cold guests to warmer tables, bringing complimentary cheesecake for celebrations, and generally treating guests like family.

What's the veterans table at Cousin Pat's?

The restaurant has a "table set for one" that honors all veterans in a meaningful tribute. The patriotic theme throughout the restaurant reflects Pat's respect for military service. Many guests find the atmosphere genuinely touching.

What should I order at Cousin Pat's?

Stick to the pub classics: fish & chips (most-praised dish), truffle mac & cheese, poutine, cheese curds, and burgers. The BBQ ribs and smoked chicken are solid. Skip the pizza unless it's late and you need carbs - quality can be inconsistent.

Is Cousin Pat's worth it?

At 4.2 stars from 1,062+ reviews, it's a solid option - especially for late night. Don't expect fine dining. Do expect decent pub food, cold drinks, sports on TV, and genuine hospitality. The value is really in the atmosphere and hours rather than culinary excellence.
