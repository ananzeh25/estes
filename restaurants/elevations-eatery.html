What Makes Elevations Special

Elevations Eatery and Bar sits right on downtown Elkhorn Avenue, making it one of the most convenient dining spots for visitors exploring Estes Park's main shopping district. The restaurant operates as a casual bar and grill rather than fine dining, but the food quality consistently exceeds expectations for the laid-back atmosphere.

With 563+ Google reviews averaging 4.4 stars, the restaurant delivers consistently good meals at reasonable prices.

The elk stew stands out as the signature dish‚Äîa mind-blowing creation that earns consistent praise. Wild game options extend to bison burgers and elk burgers, sourcing local Colorado ingredients.

The kitchen handles special dietary needs well, with clearly labeled vegetarian dishes and gluten-free options including gluten-free burger buns.

Service receives frequent praise across reviews, with servers named Ksusha, Brad, Carlos, Nayen, Ivy, and Marija specifically mentioned for excellent service.

The restaurant stays open later than many Estes Park spots, with 8pm closing making it valuable for travelers arriving after peak dinner hours when other restaurants have closed.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Elk Stew - This signature dish delivers hearty, mind-blowing flavor worth ordering every visit.

The stew features tender elk meat in a rich broth, perfect after a day hiking in Rocky Mountain National Park. The hearty flavor ranks among the very best elk preparations available.

Bison Burger ($18) - The Classic Bison Burger comes with mushrooms, Swiss cheese, and caramelized onions on a brioche bun. The burger delivers juicy, flavorful meat, making it one of the most-ordered items.

The burger comes with lettuce, tomato, onions, and pickles on the side, and you can add fries, garden salad, or fruit for $3.

Santa Fe Chicken Burger ($17) - The house-made pepper jack and pepper hash cheese sauce makes this burger exceptional. The sauce delivers amazing flavor that stands out enough to justify ordering the burger just for the sauce.

The sauce also works perfectly as a dip for the restaurant's highly-praised french fries.

Rainbow Trout - Fresh trout delivers consistently good results. The restaurant sources local fish when possible, and the trout arrives cooked properly with good seasoning and sides.

The trout earns repeat visits from diners who return specifically for this dish.

üí° Pro Tip: The french fries at Elevations earn consistent praise‚Äîfantastic, crispy, well-seasoned, and perfect with that pepper hash cheese sauce. Order them as your side ($7) or with a burger for $3 extra.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Elevations excels for post-hiking meals when you're hungry after exploring Rocky Mountain National Park and want downtown convenience. The casual bar atmosphere works well for travelers who want quality food without dressing up or dealing with formal service.

Late diners appreciate the 8pm closing when other Estes Park restaurants have already shut down. Vegetarians and vegans find clearly labeled options including the Mediterranean Bowl and Roasted Squash Bowl.

The location makes it perfect for combining dinner with downtown shopping or catching a movie at the nearby Historic Park Theater. Looking for more dining options? Check out Bird & Jim Restaurant for wild game, The Hive Brewery for craft beer, or Ember Restaurant for lake views.

Not ideal for: Fine dining seekers will find the atmosphere too casual. Photos suggest upscale dining, but you'll discover a bar and grill instead (though food quality may pleasantly surprise you).

The downtown Elkhorn Avenue location creates parking challenges‚Äîfinding spots can prove difficult. Budget diners ordering appetizers may find portions small. The $20 cheese plate runs underwhelming with brown apple slices.

The decor runs lackluster and uninspired, though the food compensates for the ambiance limitations.

The restaurant handles groups well with suitable seating for all group sizes. Wheelchair accessibility appears available.

The moderate to quiet noise level allows easy conversation. Service quality stays consistent with prompt, friendly staff handling special requests and dietary restrictions without issues.

Practical Information

Reservations: The restaurant offers online reservations through their website (elevationseatery.com) and third-party booking sites. Making reservations just to be safe gets you seated immediately, even when tables remain open.

For downtown convenience and peace of mind during peak summer and weekend dinner hours, book ahead. Walk-ins work but may face waits during prime 6-7:30pm dining times.

Parking strategy: Street parking on Elkhorn Avenue runs paid during business hours, then switches to free after 5pm. Parking fills quickly and can prove somewhat difficult to find during peak hours.

A larger free parking lot sits within walking distance. Arrive early for lunch or plan to walk a few blocks from peripheral parking areas during peak times.

Best times to visit: Lunch (11am-2pm) typically has better parking availability and shorter waits. The kitchen maintains the same menu and pricing throughout the day, so lunch offers good value without dinner crowds.

Late dining works well here since they stay open until 8pm when many Estes Park restaurants close earlier. The late hours prove wise in a tourist town where dining options shrink after dark.

Special features: The craft cocktail menu receives consistent praise, with signature drinks like the Smoked Oaxacan (served in an oak smoking box), Smoked Old Fashioned, Lilikoi Spritz, and Lemon Drop Martini mentioned positively.

The extensive spirits selection includes bourbon, whiskey, and scotch options. Bartenders Carlos and Nayen get named specifically for excellent craft cocktails. The restaurant also offers 2-for-1 mimosas that draw brunch crowds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Elevations Eatery

How much does dinner cost at Elevations?

Expect $20-30 per person for most meals. Burgers run $17-19, salads and bowls $17-24. Add $3 for fries or salad sides. Appetizers and cocktails increase the total. Spending $50-100 per person happens when you add drinks and appetizers like the ahi tartare. The pricing matches the quality delivered.

Do I need reservations?

Reservations help but aren't always required. Online reservations get you seated immediately, even when open tables are visible. For dinner Friday-Saturday or peak summer weekends, book through their website or call (970) 586-6900. Lunch typically accommodates walk-ins more easily.

Where do I park?

Downtown Elkhorn Avenue parking runs paid during business hours, free after 5pm. Parking fills quickly, so arrive early or walk from nearby lots. A big free parking lot sits not too far on foot. Street parking exists but fills quickly during peak tourist season.

What is Elevations known for?

The elk stew dominates reviews with 28 mentions, consistently called "mind-blowing" and "amazing." Wild game options including bison burgers (23 mentions) and elk burgers (20 mentions) define the menu. The restaurant also earns praise for staying open late (until 8pm) when other Estes Park spots close earlier, and for excellent craft cocktails made by skilled bartenders.

Is the atmosphere casual or fine dining?

Definitively casual. Photos suggest fine dining, but you'll find a bar and grill atmosphere with excellent food quality. The noise level runs moderate with a relaxed vibe. The decor leans lackluster and uninspired, but the food and service compensate for the ambiance limitations.

What are the vegetarian/vegan options?

Multiple clearly labeled vegetarian and vegan options exist. The Mediterranean Bowl and Roasted Squash Bowl both come vegan and gluten-free. The Blackberry Salad and Fennel & Orange Salad offer vegan options. The menu labels vegetarian dishes clearly, making this a strong choice for vegetarians. The kitchen accommodates special dietary requests.

Do they have gluten-free options?

Yes. Gluten-free burger buns cost $2 extra. Several bowls and salads come naturally gluten-free and are labeled (GF) on the menu. The gluten-free burger option provides solid choices for diners with celiac disease or gluten sensitivities.

How fast is the service?

Service runs consistently fast. Food arrives quickly from the kitchen. Service quality earns 5-star ratings, with servers delivering friendly, efficient, attentive, and prompt service. The bartenders also deliver quick, skilled cocktail preparation.

Is it good for families with kids?

Yes. The restaurant offers a kids menu ($12-13) with chicken tenders, mac & cheese, grilled cheese, and butter-parmesan noodles. The casual atmosphere makes families comfortable. The spacious storefront layout gives active children room to move, creating a relaxed environment for families.

What are the best cocktails to try?

The Smoked Oaxacan arrives in an oak smoking box for dramatic presentation. Reviewers also praise the Smoked Old Fashioned, Lemon Drop Martini (described as "tart, cold, and exactly what the moment called for"), Lilikoi Spritz, and Tropical Sour. Bartenders Carlos and Nayen get named specifically for excellent craft cocktails. The 2-for-1 mimosas draw brunch crowds.
