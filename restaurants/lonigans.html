What Makes Lonigans Special

Lonigans occupies a unique spot in Estes Park: a downtown Irish pub serving wild game burgers until midnight on weekends. With 4,173+ reviews averaging 4.4 stars, it's one of the most-reviewed restaurants in town - a testament to consistent pub food and late-night availability.

The real draw is timing. When most Estes Park restaurants close by 9pm, Lonigans keeps pouring until midnight on Fridays and Saturdays. Pool tables, foosball, and karaoke nights add entertainment value that goes beyond just eating. For post-hiking groups who want to unwind, this hits the mark.

The elk burger stands out - it's mentioned in over 100 reviews as a signature dish. At $17.50, it competes favorably with other wild game spots in town. The Irish Mac & Cheese topped with Guinness-marinated brisket delivers hearty comfort food, and the Irish Nachos (potato chips instead of tortillas) offer a twist worth trying.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Elk Burger ($17.50) - The most-mentioned item on the menu. Lean elk patty cooked to order with standard burger toppings and fries. Order medium-rare to medium - elk dries out when overcooked. A Colorado wild game experience at pub prices.

Irish Mac & Cheese ($17.95) - Homemade Irish cheese sauce with Guinness-marinated brisket on top. Rich and filling - a meal on its own. The beer cheese pairing tip: add the beer cheese dip on top for extra decadence.

Irish Nachos ($14.95) - Crispy fried potato chips (not tortillas) with wolverine melted cheese, corned beef, and pico de gallo. A shareable appetizer that stands apart from standard nachos.

Shepherd's Pie ($18.50) - Traditional beef, carrots, corn, peas, mashed potatoes with puff pastry and melted cheese. Arrives hot with flaky crust. Comfort food done right.

Prime Rib Sliders ($15.95) - Angus roast with Au Jus and caramelized onions on sweet potato buns. A more refined appetizer option that delivers on flavor.

üí° Pro Tip: The sampler tray ($20.95) lets you try multiple appetizers without commitment. Great for groups who can't decide. Pair with a Guinness on draft for the full Irish pub experience.

The Honest Take

At 4.4 stars, Lonigans delivers solid pub fare but with some inconsistency. The elk and bison burgers are reliably good, while the Irish Mac & Cheese can vary - sometimes the noodles come out slightly overcooked. Food quality is good, not exceptional.

Service can be hit or miss during busy periods. When packed for karaoke nights or weekend evenings, servers get stretched thin. Off-peak visits tend to have better service. The atmosphere leans more sports bar than traditional Irish pub - expect TVs, games, and varied music rather than fiddles and stained glass.

The value proposition is strong. At $10-20 per person for wild game burgers and Irish comfort food in a downtown location open late - that's hard to beat in Estes Park. Come for the convenience and late hours, stay for the elk burger and pool tables.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Late-night diners who need food after 9pm, groups wanting pool/foosball and games, wild game burger fans on a budget, karaoke enthusiasts, and anyone wanting pub atmosphere on downtown Elkhorn Ave. For a quieter wild game experience, try Bird & Jim instead.

Skip if: You want a traditional Irish pub atmosphere, need quiet conversation, are visiting on Tuesday (closed), or expect fine dining execution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Lonigans

What time does Lonigans close?

Lonigans stays open until midnight on Fridays and Saturdays - one of the latest kitchens in Estes Park. On Wednesday, Thursday, Sunday, and Monday, it closes at 10pm. Closed Tuesdays.

Does Lonigans have elk burgers?

Yes. The Elk Burger ($17.50) is the most popular item on the menu, mentioned in over 100 reviews. Lonigans also offers bison ($17.95) and buffalo ($17.50) burgers. Order elk medium-rare to medium - it dries out when overcooked.

What are Irish Nachos?

Irish Nachos ($14.95) replace tortilla chips with crispy fried potato chips, topped with wolverine cheese sauce, corned beef, and pico de gallo. A signature Lonigans twist on traditional nachos worth trying.

Does Lonigans have pool tables?

Yes. Lonigans has pool tables and foosball available for guests. Karaoke nights and sports on multiple TVs add to the entertainment options. It's more sports bar than traditional Irish pub in atmosphere.

Is Lonigans good for groups?

Yes. Booths fit 5 comfortably and the space accommodates groups of all sizes. The sampler tray ($20.95) is perfect for sharing. Call ahead for very large parties. The games and entertainment make it ideal for group outings.

Does Lonigans have gluten-free options?

Yes. Gluten-free buns are available for an extra $2.95. There's also a vegetarian black bean burger ($13.50) that's all-vegan with vegan toppings. Ask about specific dish modifications.

Where do I park for Lonigans?

Street parking is available on Elkhorn Avenue, though it fills up during peak hours. Public parking lots are located nearby in downtown Estes Park. Arrive early on weekends for easier parking.

Is Lonigans worth it?

At 4.4 stars from 4,173+ reviews and $10-20 per person, Lonigans delivers solid value for wild game pub food with late-night hours. The elk burger is the standout. Best for groups wanting entertainment and food after 9pm - not the choice for quiet dining or traditional Irish pub atmosphere.
