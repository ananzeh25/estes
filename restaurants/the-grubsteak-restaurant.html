What Makes The Grubsteak Special

The Grubsteak Restaurant stands as one of the few Estes Park establishments offering yak meat burgers alongside other exotic game options. With 2,675+ Google reviews averaging 4.0 stars, the restaurant maintains consistent quality despite mixed service reviews.

The downtown Elkhorn Avenue location puts diners steps from main street shopping while providing unexpected outdoor patio seating hidden from street view. Many visitors discover the back patio only after walking past the restaurant entrance.

Wild game meat variety distinguishes The Grubsteak from typical burger joints. Yak, elk, bison, buffalo, and wild boar options let adventurous eaters compare flavors across species. The yak burger earns consistent praise as leaner than beef while maintaining juicy texture and rich flavor.

The cash payment incentive drives value-conscious diners toward cash transactions. Receive a free dessert (key lime pie or Mississippi mud recommended) when paying cash, effectively reducing meal costs by $7-8 per party.

Service quality varies significantly based on timing and staffing. Some servers deliver friendly, attentive service while others seem disengaged during busy periods. Expect potential slow service, forgotten drinks, and long waits during peak hours.

The rustic mountain decor creates casual, comfortable atmosphere with old photos and woody elements. The outdoor patio provides quieter dining away from downtown foot traffic during warmer months.

üí° Pro Tip: Request outdoor patio seating when calling for reservations or upon arrival. The back patio remains hidden from street view, so many walk-ins don't realize outdoor seating exists. Patio dining provides mountain views, dog-friendly space, and quieter atmosphere compared to the front dining room.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Yak Burger - The undisputed signature item and the dish that defines The Grubsteak. This exotic burger uses lean yak meat that delivers flavor without greasiness. The meat tastes similar to beef with slightly sweeter, richer notes and less fat content.

Order the yak burger cooked medium to maintain juiciness, as the lean meat can dry out when overcooked. Add pepper jack cheese and guacamole for the most popular configuration. The burger comes with fries or substitute onion rings for extra charge.

Elk Burger ($19) - Second most popular wild game burger after the yak. The elk delivers gamey flavor more pronounced than yak or bison. Request medium or medium-rare to avoid the "hockey puck" texture that results from overcooked elk.

Meatloaf (~$20) - Traditional comfort food and the most popular non-burger entree. The meatloaf provides familiar flavors for diners hesitant about wild game while maintaining generous portions.

Bison Burger (~$18) - Middle ground between beef and elk for those new to wild game. Bison tastes closer to traditional beef than yak or elk, making it ideal first-time wild game choice. The meat maintains more moisture than elk when cooked medium or medium-well.

Shepherd's Pie (~$18) - Hearty Irish-style casserole providing a solid non-burger option with generous portions. The dish works well for post-hiking hunger with filling potato topping and meat base.

Wild Boar Scotch Eggs (~$12) - Unique appetizer wrapping hard-boiled eggs in wild boar sausage with crispy breading. Order these to share before main courses arrive.

Buffalo Ribeye ($30-35) - Premium buffalo steak delivers leaner alternative to beef ribeye. The meat comes out juicy and tender when cooked medium-rare. Some cuts contain gristle, but flavor quality remains consistent.

Chicken Wings (~$13) - Unexpected standout often called "some of the best wings ever." The restaurant offers multiple sauce options including buffalo, BBQ, and specialty flavors. The blue cheese dressing adds quality finishing touch.

The Wild Game Experience

The outdoor patio creates the ideal setting for sampling exotic meats. Request patio seating and order the yak burger alongside wild boar scotch eggs to compare wild game flavors. The casual atmosphere removes pressure for formal dining behavior.

Happy hour (approximately 3-6pm) drops craft beer prices to $5, making beer sampling more affordable when pairing with wild game burgers. The bartenders pour local Colorado beers that complement gamey flavors better than standard domestic options.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Adventurous eaters seeking unique protein experiences find ideal variety at The Grubsteak. The yak, elk, bison, buffalo, and wild boar options create tasting adventures unavailable at chain restaurants.

Post-hiking diners appreciate the downtown location just blocks from main street parking. The generous portions and hearty menu items refuel calories burned on Rocky Mountain National Park trails.

Budget-conscious diners benefit from the cash payment dessert incentive and competitive $20-30 per person pricing for Estes Park standards. Happy hour beer deals extend value further.

Families with kids work well with the casual atmosphere and varied menu including standard beef burgers and chicken options alongside exotic meats. The outdoor patio accommodates families with young children and dogs.

Looking for more Estes Park dining? Check out Bird & Jim Restaurant for wild game in upscale mountain cabin setting, The Hive Brewery for craft beer and bison short ribs, or Trailhead Restaurant for elk stew near Rocky Mountain National Park entrance.

Not ideal for: Diners requiring fast service during busy periods should reconsider. Expect 15+ minute waits for seating, slow drink refills, and lengthy food delivery times during peak hours.

The service quality inconsistency creates unpredictable experiences. Some servers deliver excellent attention while others seem disengaged or overwhelmed. The experience depends heavily on which server you receive and restaurant timing.

Vegetarians and vegans find limited options beyond Beyond Burger and basic salads. The restaurant clearly focuses on meat-centric dishes with token vegetarian items rather than thoughtful plant-based cuisine.

Diners seeking upscale fine dining atmosphere should look elsewhere. The Grubsteak operates as casual, somewhat dated steak house rather than modern restaurant. Ember Restaurant provides fine dining with Lake Estes views for special occasions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about The Grubsteak Restaurant

Is the yak burger really worth trying?

Yes. The yak burger defines The Grubsteak's identity and remains the most ordered item. Yak tastes similar to beef with sweeter, richer notes and significantly less fat. The lean meat stays juicy when cooked medium. Order it with pepper jack and guacamole for the most popular configuration. This unique protein experience isn't available at most restaurants.

How much does a typical meal cost?

Expect $20-30 per person including entree and drink. Burgers range $17-20, premium entrees like buffalo ribeye reach $30-35, and appetizers cost $10-15. The pricing matches typical Estes Park restaurant rates. Pay cash to receive free dessert worth $7-8, effectively reducing per-person costs.

What's the service quality like?

Service varies significantly. Some staff deliver friendly, attentive service while others seem disengaged. Expect slow seating (15+ minute waits), potentially forgotten drinks, and inattentive service during busy periods. Service quality depends heavily on timing and which server you receive. Off-peak hours generally provide better service.

Where do I park?

Free parking lot behind the restaurant provides easiest access via side streets off Elkhorn Avenue. Street parking on Elkhorn requires metered payment and fills quickly during summer. The back lot offers more availability and eliminates parking fees.

Can I eat outside with my dog?

Yes. The outdoor patio in back allows dogs and provides water bowls upon request. Many visitors don't realize the patio exists because it's hidden from street view. Always request patio seating when arriving, as servers may not automatically mention it. The patio offers mountain views and quieter atmosphere away from downtown foot traffic.

What should I order besides the yak burger?

Meatloaf ranks as top non-burger entree. Chicken wings earn praise as "best wings ever." Wild boar scotch eggs provide unique appetizer experience. Bison burger delivers wild game flavor closer to beef than elk for first-time exotic meat eaters. Buffalo ribeye works for premium steak seekers wanting leaner alternative to beef.

Do they have vegetarian options?

Limited. Beyond Burger appears on menu alongside basic salads and vegetarian sides. The restaurant clearly focuses on meat-centric dishes rather than thoughtful plant-based cuisine. The Alfredo fettuccine and Beyond Burger work as decent options, but vegetarians should expect token options rather than extensive choices.

When is happy hour?

Approximately 3-6pm based on customer reports. Craft draft beers drop to $5 during happy hour, making beer sampling more affordable. The bar serves local Colorado beers that pair well with wild game burgers. Sit at the bar for fastest service and best happy hour experience.

How should I order game meat burgers cooked?

Medium or medium-rare for yak, elk, and buffalo burgers. Lean game meats dry out quickly when overcooked, resulting in "hockey puck" texture from well-done burgers. Trust the kitchen's recommendation. However, cooking temperatures can run inconsistent, so inspect your burger when it arrives and send back if overcooked. The flavor difference between properly cooked medium and overcooked well-done drastically affects game meat enjoyment.

Is it good for post-hiking meals?

Yes. The downtown location sits just blocks from main street parking, making it convenient stop after Rocky Mountain National Park hikes. Generous portions and hearty menu items refuel burned calories. The outdoor patio provides casual atmosphere suitable for hiking attire. Open daily through dinner hours unlike some restaurants that close mid-afternoon.
