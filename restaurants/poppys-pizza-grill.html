What Makes Poppy's Special

Poppy's Pizza & Grill occupies one of the best locations in Estes Park - right on the Riverwalk with outdoor seating overlooking the Big Thompson River. With 2,773+ reviews averaging 4.1 stars, it's a consistent performer for families and groups looking for casual riverside dining.

The setting does the heavy lifting. On a warm afternoon, the patio fills up with families watching the river flow by, kids finishing pizza slices while parents enjoy a cold beer. The mountain backdrop completes the quintessential Estes Park experience.

The kitchen handles high volume well during peak season. Service stays friendly even when the patio is packed. Large groups are welcome - the space accommodates parties without feeling cramped.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Straziante Pizza ($23.99 large) - The house specialty. Italian sausage with roasted peppers, onions, and garlic creates a savory combination with good flavor depth. The toppings are generous without overwhelming the crust.

Green Chile Pizza ($22.99 large) - Colorado meets Italy. Roasted green chile with chicken and jalape√±os brings that Southwestern kick. A local favorite that you won't find at every pizzeria.

Calzone ($14.99) - Stuffed with ricotta and mozzarella, choice of three toppings. The portion is substantial - easily shareable or a hearty solo meal. Comes with marinara for dipping.

Chicken Parmesan ($17.99) - For those not in the pizza mood. Breaded chicken with marinara and melted mozzarella over pasta. Comfort food done right.

üí° Pro Tip: Order large pizzas for the best crust experience. Large pies are hand-tossed and made fresh, while individual-sized pizzas may use pre-made crusts. The large easily feeds 3-4 people and represents better value.

The Honest Take

At 4.1 stars, Poppy's delivers solid pizza in an exceptional location. The food is good but not remarkable - it's the Riverwalk setting that elevates the experience. Come for the patio and mountain views, and you'll leave satisfied.

The crust varies based on what you order. Large pizzas get the fresh hand-tossed treatment, while personal pies can feel more generic. Stick to the large sizes when ordering pizza. The calzones are consistently good across the board.

Peak season brings crowds, which means occasional service slowdowns. The kitchen handles volume well, but expect longer waits when the patio is full. This is typical for any popular Riverwalk restaurant during summer months.

Gluten-free crust is available for those with dietary restrictions. Vegetarian options are plentiful with the veggie pizza and pasta selections.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Families with kids who want pizza by the river, groups looking for patio seating with mountain views, post-Riverwalk strolling, casual dinner after a day of hiking, and anyone wanting a relaxed dining experience.

Skip if: You're seeking authentic Italian thin-crust, want a quiet intimate dinner (the patio gets lively), need quick service during peak season, or prefer ordering individual personal pizzas (go large here).

Looking for other downtown dining? Check Himalayan Curry & Kebob for something different or Big Horn Restaurant for breakfast.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Poppy's Pizza & Grill

What is Poppy's best pizza?

The Straziante pizza is the house signature - Italian sausage with roasted peppers, onions, and garlic. The Green Chile pizza is a Colorado favorite with roasted green chile, chicken, and jalape√±os. Both are best ordered as large pies for fresh hand-tossed crust.

Does Poppy's have outdoor seating?

Yes - the riverside patio is the main attraction. Seating overlooks the Big Thompson River with mountain views. The patio fills up quickly during peak season (summer afternoons especially). Weather permitting only.

Is Poppy's good for kids?

Very kid-friendly. The kids menu includes cheese pizza, spaghetti, chicken fingers, and grilled cheese for $7-9. The casual patio atmosphere is relaxed and forgiving. Kids love watching the river while waiting for food.

How much does pizza cost at Poppy's?

Large 16" pizzas run $19-25. Build your own starts at $18.99 plus $2.50 per topping. Personal pies are available but large pizzas offer better value and fresh hand-tossed crust. Two people can share a large pizza with salad for about $15-18 each.

Does Poppy's have gluten-free options?

Yes - gluten-free pizza crust is available for an additional charge. Many salads and grill items can also accommodate gluten-free diets. Ask your server about specific modifications.

Can Poppy's handle large groups?

Yes - the patio and indoor space accommodate large groups well. For parties of 8 or more, calling ahead helps secure seating. The kitchen handles high volume efficiently. Large pizza orders can be prepared in advance with notice.

Where is Poppy's located in Estes Park?

342 E Elkhorn Ave, right on the Riverwalk in downtown Estes Park. It's an easy walk from the main visitor parking areas and part of the scenic Riverwalk path along the Big Thompson River.

Is Poppy's Pizza worth it?

For the location and experience, yes. At 4.1 stars from 2,773+ reviews with $10-30 pricing, Poppy's delivers reliable pizza in one of Estes Park's best dining locations. The Riverwalk patio with river and mountain views makes it more than just a pizza stop. Order large pizzas for the best food experience.
