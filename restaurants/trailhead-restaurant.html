What Makes Trailhead Special

Trailhead sits just outside Rocky Mountain National Park entrance on Fall River Road, making it the most convenient dining stop before or after park visits. With 633+ reviews averaging 4.3 stars, the outdoor patio delivers spectacular mountain views that enhance every meal.

The Elk Stew stands as the signature dish - thick stew with elk meat, vegetables, and seasonings that earns consistent praise. The integrated gift shop offers a unique perk: shop first and get 15% off your entire meal.

üí° Pro Tip: I'd always shop the gift shop first and grab your receipt before ordering food. The 15% meal discount applies to your entire order including drinks - that's $5-10 saved per person on a family meal. The discount never expires, so take your time browsing.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Elk Stew ($24.99) - If you're only ordering one thing, this is it. Thick stew with tender elk meat, vegetables, and seasonings served with sour cream. The hearty portion works perfectly after cold mornings in the park.

The kitchen adjusts spice levels and customizes based on dietary needs. I'd order this on chilly days for maximum comfort food satisfaction.

Mountain Man Burger ($22.99) - Five game meats (elk, venison, wild boar, antelope, buffalo) combine into one wild burger. I'd go medium rare or medium for the best texture.

Trust me on the cook temp - lean game meat dries out fast. Well-done game burgers turn into hockey pucks. Go medium unless you specifically want otherwise.

Rocky Mountain Trout ($32.99) - Boneless rainbow trout with lemon caper butter sauce, wild rice, and saut√©ed vegetables. The dish stands out for perfect cooking and excellent seasoning, bringing diners back repeatedly.

The fresh local trout arrives properly prepared without the muddy flavor that plagues poorly-handled fish. The lemon caper butter adds brightness without overwhelming the delicate fish flavor.

Grilled Pork Chops ($29.95) - Bone-in pork chop with apple cranberry compote, mashed potatoes, and saut√©ed vegetables. The sweet compote balances the savory pork perfectly, creating a dish that earns 5-star ratings consistently.

Portion sizes run generous, often too much to finish in one sitting. The quality exceeds expectations for a location-driven restaurant near a national park.

Shrimp Scampi ($25.99) - Shrimp and linguini tossed in scampi sauce with garlic and parmesan. The dish brings repeat customers back with perfectly cooked shrimp and a light but flavorful sauce.

üí° Pro Tip: Real talk - I'd skip the regular beef hamburgers and sandwiches. They tend to run dry even when ordered medium. Stick to the Elk Stew, game burgers (ordered medium rare/medium), trout, pork chops, or pasta dishes which deliver consistent quality.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: RMNP visitors needing pre-hike breakfast or post-hike meals, families (patio wildlife sightings, gift shop for kids), gluten-free diners, and gift shop browsers who want the 15% meal discount.

Skip if: You need dinner (closes 4-5pm), want consistent beef burgers (stick to elk stew/game burgers), or dislike retail-heavy atmosphere.

Looking for other options? Check Bird & Jim for wild game or Elevations Eatery for late-night dining.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Trailhead Restaurant

How much does a meal cost at Trailhead?

Expect $20-30 per person for most meals. Burgers and sandwiches run $17.99-22.99, with game burgers at the higher end. Specialties range $24.99-39.95, with Elk Stew at $24.99 and Rib Eye Steak at $39.95. Pasta dishes fall in the $25.99-28.99 range. Add sides, drinks, and potential starter to reach $25-35 per person for full meals.

Do I need reservations?

No. Reservations not required and walk-ins receive immediate seating most times with no wait even during busy periods. The restaurant rarely fills to capacity. Just arrive during operating hours (10am-4pm weekdays, 9am-5pm weekends) and expect to be seated quickly.

How does the gift shop discount work?

Shop the two-floor gift shop before ordering food. Make any purchase (even small items like magnets or postcards) and keep your receipt. Show the gift shop receipt when ordering your meal and receive 15% off your entire food bill including drinks. The receipt never expires, so you can browse leisurely. On a $80 meal, you save $12.

What are the signature dishes?

Elk Stew ($24.99) stands as the house specialty. Thick stew with elk meat, vegetables, and seasonings served with sour cream. Game burgers (Buffalo, Elk, Mountain Man at $22.99 each) provide unique alternatives. Rocky Mountain Trout ($32.99) and Grilled Pork Chops ($29.95) deliver consistent quality. Avoid standard beef burgers which run inconsistent.

Where do I park?

Free parking lot with plenty of spaces available. No parking fees unlike some Estes Park locations. The convenient lot makes this an easy stop when driving to/from Rocky Mountain National Park entrances on Fall River Road.

Can I eat outside with mountain views?

Yes. Outdoor patio seating provides spectacular mountain views that significantly enhance the dining experience. Request patio seating when weather permits. Indoor window seating maintains views during cold or windy conditions. Outdoor diners often spot wildlife including hummingbirds, magpies, and rabbits.

What are the hours?

Monday-Friday: 10am-4pm. Saturday-Sunday: 9am-5pm. This breakfast-and-lunch schedule means no dinner service. Plan accordingly if visiting after full Rocky Mountain National Park days. Best for pre-park breakfast or post-hike lunch rather than evening dining.

Do they have gluten-free options?

Yes. Gluten-free chili stands out for excellent taste and safe preparation. Gluten-free bread available for burger substitution. The kitchen staff works directly with dietary restrictions including vegan modifications and handles strict dietary needs well.

How far is it from Rocky Mountain National Park?

Located at 3450 Fall River Rd, just outside RMNP entrance. This makes Trailhead the most convenient dining stop before entering or after exiting the park. Perfect for breakfast before timed entry permits or lunch after morning hikes without driving into downtown Estes Park.

Should I order burgers medium or well-done?

Medium or medium rare for game burgers (elk, buffalo, mountain man). Lean game meat turns dry and tough when overcooked, resulting in "hockey puck" texture. Trust the kitchen's recommendation. However, cooking runs inconsistent even when ordering medium, particularly for standard beef burgers. Consider ordering Elk Stew, trout, pork chops, or pasta instead if burger quality concerns you.
