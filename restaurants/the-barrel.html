What Makes The Barrel Special

The Barrel is Estes Park's premier beer garden - 64 taps of craft beer and cider in a massive outdoor space with gorgeous planters and mountain views. Located just two blocks off main street on Moraine Ave, it draws locals and visitors who want variety, atmosphere, and the freedom to bring their own food.

The no-kitchen model works. Rotating food trucks provide BBQ, tacos, and other options right on the premises. Prefer something else? Bring food from any Estes Park restaurant or pack a picnic. This flexibility means beer and food pairing is entirely in your hands.

For craft beer enthusiasts, the selection is unmatched in town. Colorado breweries dominate the tap list, with seasonal offerings like pumpkin cider and imperial stouts. The full bar includes an extensive whiskey and bourbon collection. Flights let you sample without committing to full pints.

What to Order (Best Picks)

Beer Flights - The move for first-timers. Sample 4-6 beers from the 64-tap selection. The bartenders are knowledgeable and can guide you toward Colorado IPAs, sours, stouts, or ciders based on your preferences.

Rusty Nail Imperial Stout - A standout on the tap list. Rich, robust, and perfect for cooler mountain evenings.

Colorado Peach Cider - A crowd favorite seasonal option. Refreshing after a day hiking RMNP.

Bloody Mary with Pickled Green Beans - The house version comes garnished with house-pickled green beans. A legit cocktail option for non-beer drinkers.

Food Truck BBQ - The rotating trucks are consistently good. BBQ pulled pork, street tacos, and specialty items depend on which truck is on-site. Check the website or ask at the bar.

üí° Pro Tip: Ask for a flight to sample the 64-tap selection before committing. The bartenders know the menu and can build a flight based on what you like. Dog owners: they'll bring a fresh water bowl for your pup.

The Honest Take

At 4.4 stars from 920 reviews, The Barrel delivers exactly what it promises: the best craft beer selection in Estes Park in a beautiful outdoor setting. The bartenders are friendly and knowledgeable. The atmosphere is relaxed and welcoming.

The trade-off is no kitchen. If no food truck is on-site or you don't want to bring your own food, you're limited to bar snacks (jerky, peanuts). The beer menu screens rotate quickly - some visitors find them hard to read. Parking fills up during festivals and busy weekends.

The venue excels for private events. Wedding receptions, rehearsal dinners, and group gatherings get dedicated space with room for 100+ guests. For casual visitors, expect a beer-focused experience rather than a full restaurant.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Craft beer enthusiasts who want variety, dog owners seeking a welcoming patio, post-RMNP groups wanting to relax outdoors, and event planners looking for wedding reception or group party venues. Compare to The Hive if you want a full kitchen with your craft beer.

Skip if: You need a full menu (no kitchen here), want to dine with small kids (more bar-oriented), or are visiting when no food truck is on-site and don't want to bring food.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about The Barrel

Does The Barrel serve food?

The Barrel doesn't have a kitchen. Food comes from rotating food trucks on-site (BBQ, tacos, etc.) or you can bring your own food from any Estes Park restaurant. Delivery is also welcome. Bar snacks like jerky and nuts are available.

How many beers does The Barrel have on tap?

64 taps - the largest craft beer selection in Estes Park. Options include Colorado IPAs, stouts, lagers, sours, and seasonal ciders. Flights are available for sampling multiple beers.

Is The Barrel dog-friendly?

Yes. Dogs are welcome on the outdoor patio and even inside. The staff provides fresh water bowls for every dog. It's a popular spot for dog owners in Estes Park.

Can I host a private event at The Barrel?

Yes. The Barrel regularly hosts wedding receptions, rehearsal dinners, and group events for 100+ people. They have indoor reserved areas and the massive outdoor patio. Contact them in advance to book private events.

Where do I park for The Barrel?

There's a free parking lot on-site. During festivals and busy weekends, the lot can fill up - in which case street parking and downtown lots are nearby. The venue is just 2 blocks off main street.

Does The Barrel have cocktails?

Yes. Beyond 64 beer taps, there's a full bar with an extensive whiskey and bourbon selection, plus craft cocktails. The Bloody Mary with pickled green beans is a standout. Margaritas and specialty drinks are available.

What food trucks are at The Barrel?

Food trucks rotate regularly - past trucks have included BBQ, Mexican street tacos, and other options. Check the website or call ahead to see which truck is on-site. You can also bring food from nearby restaurants.

Is The Barrel worth it?

For craft beer lovers, absolutely. At 4.4 stars from 920 reviews, The Barrel delivers the best beer selection in Estes Park in a beautiful outdoor setting. The no-kitchen model means flexibility - pair any beer with food truck fare or BYOF. Perfect for post-hike relaxation.
