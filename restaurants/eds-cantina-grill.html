What Makes Ed's Cantina Special

Ed's Cantina occupies prime real estate on Elkhorn Avenue right along the riverwalk. The outdoor patio puts you next to the river with mountain views and a festive atmosphere - the best outdoor dining location on the main strip.

The kitchen focuses on sustainable, locally-sourced ingredients including Colorado bison and beef. With 3,443+ reviews averaging 4.3 stars, Ed's draws both tourists and locals looking for Mexican food with genuine Colorado character.

Service runs fast even when busy. Food arrives quickly despite the crowds, and the staff handles large parties well. The vibrant atmosphere gets lively but stays comfortable for conversation.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Bison Enchiladas - The signature dish with 155+ specific mentions. Flavorful, well-seasoned, with the right balance of spice. The lean bison meat pairs perfectly with the sauce. Ask about heat level if you're sensitive.

Avocado Margarita - The drink that put Ed's on the map. Creamy, unique, and refreshing. The mango and spicy margaritas also deliver, but the avocado version is the one to try first.

Fish Tacos ($16) - Battered cod with cabbage and cilantro sauce on soft flour tortillas. Crispy fish, fresh toppings, well-balanced. A solid choice for seafood lovers.

Barbacoa Tacos ($15) - Shredded beef with cheese, pico, and avocado salsa on soft corn. The meat is tender and well-seasoned. Great value for the quality.

Veggie Enchiladas ($17.50) - Squash, black beans, and a white cashew-based sauce. Clearly labeled vegetarian/vegan options throughout the menu make this a standout for plant-based diners.

üí° Pro Tip: Order one taco of each kind to sample the variety. The bison, barbacoa, and fish are the standouts. Add rice and beans for $4 to make it a full meal.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Post-RMNP hikers craving Mexican with a view, groups wanting riverside patio seating, vegetarians and vegans with clearly labeled options, and anyone wanting Colorado-sourced bison in their enchiladas.

Skip if: You expect free chips and salsa ($6.50 here) or need a quiet, intimate dining experience - it gets lively.

Looking for other options? Check La Cocina De Mama for off-strip Mexican with free parking, or Wapiti Colorado Pub for prime rib with river views.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Ed's Cantina & Grill

How much does dinner cost at Ed's Cantina?

Expect $10-20 per person. Tacos run $15-16, specialties like enchiladas $18-22, and burritos $17.50-19.50. Add $6.50 for chips and salsa. Margaritas are extra. A couple can eat well for $40-50 total including drinks.

Do I need reservations at Ed's Cantina?

No reservations - waitlist only. Add your name and walk around downtown Estes Park while you wait. During peak hours, expect 30-60 minute waits. Bar seating is often available faster. They enforce a 90-minute table limit when busy.

What are the bison enchiladas like at Ed's?

The signature dish. Lean Colorado bison with flavorful sauce and the right amount of spice. The meat is well-seasoned to compensate for bison's leanness. Ask about heat level - it can have a kick. Served with rice and beans.

Is Ed's Cantina good for vegetarians?

Excellent for vegetarians and vegans. Menu items are clearly labeled VG (vegetarian), V (vegan), and GF (gluten-free). The veggie enchiladas with cashew-based white sauce stand out. Falafel tacos offer a vegan option. Staff accommodates customizations.

Does Ed's Cantina have outdoor seating?

Yes - the riverside patio is the main attraction. Request outdoor seating for river views and mountain scenery. The patio sits right on the Elkhorn Avenue riverwalk. Indoor seating and bar area also available. Outdoor fills up fast on nice days.

What's the avocado margarita like?

Creamy, unique, and the signature drink. The avocado adds richness without being overpowering. The mango and spicy margaritas also deliver. Well-balanced tart and sweet. Happy hour offers drink specials.

Where do I park for Ed's Cantina?

Small lot next to restaurant (limited spaces). Paid parking at library lot across the street. Free street parking is available after 5pm. Parking can be challenging during peak summer - arrive early or walk from your hotel if nearby.

Is Ed's Cantina good after hiking RMNP?

Perfect post-hike spot. Located right on downtown Elkhorn Ave, walkable from most hotels. The hearty bison and steak dishes refuel after long hikes. Riverside patio lets you decompress with mountain views. Service is fast even when tired and hungry.
