What Makes Latitude 105 Special

Latitude 105 sits inside the Holiday Inn, which scares off tourists expecting bland hotel food. That's a mistake. The gastropub delivers legitimate elk chili nachos, wild game burgers, and craft beer in a lodge-like setting with stone accents and mountain vibes.

The real draw is convenience without compromise. Breakfast starts at 6:30am - earlier than most downtown spots. The dinner menu offers elk and bison burgers at $10-20 per person while Elkhorn Avenue charges 30% more for the same proteins. Free parking, zero wait times, and a pool table add to the appeal.

For families, the arcade games and casual atmosphere make it easier than battling for tables downtown. The pet-friendly patio welcomes dogs, and happy hour (4-6pm) shaves dollars off an already reasonable bill.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Elk Chili Nachos ($16.99) - The signature appetizer. A mountain of chips loaded with house-made elk chili, melted cheese, jalape√±os, and sour cream. Rich, gamey flavor without being overwhelming. Easily shareable for two.

Elk Burger ($18.99) - Lean elk patty with bacon and cheddar. Order medium - elk dries out if overcooked. The bun holds up well, and the portion is satisfying without being excessive.

Poutine ($14.99) - Crispy fries, Colorado beef gravy, and real cheese curds. A solid comfort food choice, especially after a cold day hiking. Ask for extra gravy on the side.

Mile-High Mud Pie ($9.99) - Layers of chocolate, coffee ice cream, and whipped cream. Massive portion - consider splitting. The name delivers on the height.

üí° Pro Tip: Hit happy hour between 4-6pm for $2 off draft beers and $3 off cocktails. The elk nachos and a craft beer make a perfect light dinner for under $25.

The Honest Take

At 3.9 stars from 459 reviews, Latitude 105 sits below the top-tier Estes Park restaurants. The food is good, not exceptional. Expect solid gastropub fare rather than culinary innovation. Portion sizes are reasonable but not the massive plates you'll find at Big Horn or Ed's Cantina.

Service can be inconsistent - it's hotel staff doing double duty, not career servers. Peak summer weekends may see slower kitchen times. The split hours (closed 9:30am-4pm) catch some visitors off guard. But for the price point and convenience, these are minor trade-offs.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Early risers needing breakfast before RMNP, families with kids who want arcade games and casual vibes, budget-conscious diners seeking wild game at fair prices, and anyone staying at Holiday Inn. Looking for craft beer with your burger? The Hive has a better selection.

Skip if: You want downtown atmosphere, need lunch service (closed 9:30am-4pm), or expect fine dining execution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Latitude 105 Alehouse

What time does Latitude 105 open for breakfast?

Latitude 105 opens at 6:30am for breakfast - one of the earliest openings in Estes Park. Breakfast is served until 9:30am daily. This makes it ideal for early risers planning to hit RMNP before crowds.

Is Latitude 105 open for lunch?

No. Latitude 105 is closed between 9:30am and 4pm. The restaurant operates breakfast (6:30-9:30am) and dinner (4-8pm weekdays, 4-10pm Fri-Sat) only. For lunch, check downtown options like Elevations Eatery.

Does Latitude 105 have elk burgers?

Yes. The Elk Burger ($18.99) comes with bacon and cheddar on a brioche bun. There's also a Bison Burger ($17.99) and signature Elk Chili Nachos ($16.99) for those wanting wild game options without a full burger.

Is Latitude 105 good for families with kids?

Yes. The restaurant has arcade games and a pool table that keep kids entertained. The casual lodge atmosphere is family-friendly, and portions include kid-friendly options. The outdoor patio is also pet-friendly for families traveling with dogs.

Do I need reservations at Latitude 105?

Reservations are available through OpenTable but rarely necessary. As a hotel restaurant off the main tourist drag, Latitude 105 doesn't experience the long waits of downtown spots. Walk-ins are welcome and typically seated immediately.

Where do I park for Latitude 105?

Free parking is available in the Holiday Inn lot. The lot has plenty of spaces and is easy to access from St Vrain Avenue. No downtown parking hassles or fees.

Does Latitude 105 have happy hour?

Yes. Happy hour runs 4-6pm daily with $2 off draft beers and $3 off cocktails. Pair the discounted drinks with elk nachos for a budget-friendly pre-dinner or light meal.

Is Latitude 105 worth it?

For the price point, yes. At 3.9 stars and $10-20 per person, Latitude 105 delivers solid gastropub food without downtown crowds or prices. The early breakfast, free parking, happy hour, and family-friendly amenities add real value. Not a destination restaurant, but a smart choice for convenience.
