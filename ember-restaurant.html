What Makes Ember Special

Ember Restaurant & Bar combines stunning Lake Estes views with modern American fine dining that earns 4.4 stars across 368+ reviews. This contemporary restaurant sits inside the Lake Estes Marina building with floor-to-ceiling windows offering lake and mountain views from every table. The outdoor patio provides even closer access to the waterfront scenery, especially spectacular during sunset.

Service quality appears consistently across reviews. Multiple diners name servers (Max, Sebastian, Alejandra, Robert, Sam) who provide attentive service, thoughtful recommendations, and genuine care for the dining experience. The modern interior features high-quality surfaces, comfortable leather seating, and elegant design that creates upscale ambiance without feeling stuffy.

üí° Pro Tip: Request outdoor patio seating for sunset dining. Multiple reviewers mention watching elk come down to the lake during dinner, creating unforgettable mountain dining moments you can't experience elsewhere.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Steak Frites ($45) - The most-mentioned dish with 25+ reviews. Sliced bistro filet served with brown butter, candied bacon, and garlic-parmesan fries. Reviewers consistently praise the tender meat and perfectly cooked preparation. One reviewer called it "expertly prepared" while another noted "cooked per request" with excellent execution.

Lobster Bisque ($11 cup / $21 bowl) - Appears in 14+ reviews as a standout starter. Made with Maine lobster, served with tarragon crostini and chives. One diner rated it "5/5" noting "the amount of meat and wonderful flavor made this my favorite part of lunch." The bisque balances rich, creamy texture with generous lobster portions.

Elk Tenderloin ($53) - Premium wild game option mentioned in 10+ reviews. Served with lingonberry demiglace, beech mushrooms, truffle mashed potatoes, and Brussels sprouts. One anniversary diner's wife declared it "the best meal she has ever had" with "tender and tasty" meat and beautiful presentation.

Bourbon Cheesecake - The most popular dessert across reviews. Multiple diners specifically mention ordering this to finish their meal, describing it as "outstanding" and worth saving room for. Comes with a cherry base that "added to the enjoyment."

üí° Pro Tip: Start with the bread & butter ($9). Multiple reviews call this "unexpectedly amazing" with house focaccia, whipped butter, garlic confit, and black salt. One reviewer compared it favorably to bread they had in Italy.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: Ember excels for special occasions and anniversaries. Multiple reviews specifically mention celebrating anniversaries here, with the combination of views, service, and food quality creating memorable experiences. Couples seeking romantic dinners appreciate the elegant atmosphere and lakeside setting. Families work well with the kids menu and varied options. Vacationers willing to splurge find the pricing justified by the quality and setting.

Not ideal for: Budget diners should know that $30-50+ per person (drinks extra) adds up quickly. One local reviewer noted they "can really only show up for an anniversary or special occasion" due to pricing. Quick casual meals don't match the restaurant's style‚Äîexpect 60-90+ minute dining experiences. The upscale pricing doesn't align with completely casual dining expectations, though the atmosphere remains welcoming rather than stuffy.

The restaurant accommodates dietary restrictions with vegetarian options clearly marked and gluten-free dishes available. Wheelchair accessibility works with proper entry access. The dress code stays casual (hiking clothes welcome according to reviews) despite the upscale food and pricing. Both indoor and outdoor seating options let you choose your preferred experience.

Common Questions

Is Ember worth the price?

For special occasions and memorable experiences, yes. Reviews consistently mention the combination of lake views, excellent service, and quality food justifying the $30-50+ per person pricing. For casual regular dining, locals note it's "a bit on the expensive side" and better reserved for anniversaries.

Do all tables have lake views?

Yes. Multiple reviews specifically state "beautiful view of Estes Park Lake from every table" and "there really is no bad seat in the house." Request outdoor patio seating for the closest lake access and potential wildlife sightings.

Can you eat here casually in hiking clothes?

Yes, there's no strict dress code. The restaurant welcomes casual mountain attire despite the upscale food and pricing. The atmosphere is described as "intimate, cozy elegance" rather than stuffy fine dining.

Do they serve breakfast?

Yes, breakfast service starts at 7am daily. Menu includes French toast, bacon, and other breakfast items. One reviewer praised the "half order of French toast with strawberries and fresh whipped cream" as "delicious."

Is Ember kid-friendly?

Yes, the restaurant welcomes families and offers a kids menu. Multiple reviewers mention bringing children. One reviewer noted "we went with our two children and we were blown away." The setting works for families despite the upscale pricing.

What are the vegetarian/vegan options?

Vegetarian options are clearly labeled on the menu. The Squash Tacos ($23) offer a full vegan entree. Salads can be ordered without meat additions. One reviewer noted "somewhat recommend" for vegetarians, suggesting options exist but aren't the main focus.

How far in advance should I book reservations?

For dinner, especially Friday-Saturday or holidays, book as far ahead as possible. The restaurant fills up and reviewers consistently recommend reservations. Lunch may have more flexibility. Use Toast Tab online booking or call (970) 586-6409.

Can you see wildlife from the restaurant?

Yes! One detailed review mentions "two elk strolled down to the water right as the sun was setting" during their outdoor patio dinner. This creates unique "scene from a movie" experiences you can't get at typical restaurants.

What's the noise level like?

Reviews describe "moderate noise" and "quiet, easy to talk" depending on seating area and time. The atmosphere allows easy conversation without shouting. Indoor dining tends quieter than the bar area.

Do portions match the pricing?

Reviews generally indicate appropriate portions for the pricing. One reviewer noted a steak sandwich had "a lot of bread for the small amount of meat," but most dishes receive praise for generous, well-balanced portions. Appetizers like Mango Shrimp are noted as "good size."
