What Makes The Hive Special

The Hive at Estes Park Brewery combines craft brewing with quality food execution that earns 4.9 stars across 7,376+ reviews. This two-level space puts the restaurant upstairs with brewery operations visible, while downstairs features a casual bar with pool tables and free beer samples.

The brewery makes all beers in-house with popular options like raspberry wheat, gold lager, and Lazarus appearing frequently in reviews.

Service quality stands out consistently. Reviewers name servers (Batu, Irem, Yair, Susana, Lucky) who provide attentive service, helpful menu recommendations, and create welcoming experiences.

The urban-meets-mountain atmosphere feels less touristy than typical Estes Park spots. Multiple reviews mention this replaced the old Estes Park Brewery with significant improvements to food quality and ambiance.

üí° Pro Tip: Arrive during the 20-30 minute wait and head downstairs to the bar for free beer samples. The bartenders pour small tastes of their house brews so you can decide what to order with dinner. This turns wait time into a tasting experience.

What to Order (Signature Dishes)

Bison Short Ribs (~$36) - Multiple reviewers call these "AMAZING!!" and recommend getting them specifically. One review states "if you come here--get the short ribs!! 10/10‚≠êÔ∏è"

The meat comes tender with great flavor, served with mashed potatoes and green beans. Worth ordering even if you're hesitant about the higher price point.

Elk Burger (~$20) - You can upgrade any burger to elk meat for a few dollars. Reviews consistently praise this as "delightful" with 166 mentions making it one of the most-ordered items.

Leaner than beef but stays juicy. Comes with fries and coleslaw. The Sasquatch burger also appears frequently in positive reviews as a solid choice.

Rack of Lamb with Chimichurri (~$38) - Perfectly cooked and seasoned according to multiple reviews. One diner rated it "sensational" while another called it "so delicious and the shrimp is so buttery."

Comes with mashed potatoes and green beans. Several reviewers specifically mention returning to try other menu items after having the lamb.

Beer Flight (~$12) - Sample 4-5 of their house-brewed beers. Popular options include raspberry wheat beer (refreshing, subtle raspberry finish), gold lager (fresh and crisp), chocolate stout, and Lazarus.

The brewery offers complimentary small tastings at the downstairs bar, but the flight lets you try larger pours upstairs with your meal.

üí° Pro Tip: The sausage sampler (~$18) and fried green beans (~$12) work well as shared appetizers for groups. Reviews mention both come with excellent sides (pickles, mustard for sausage; truffle aioli for beans) and portions feed 2-3 people easily.

Who It's For (And Who It's Not For)

Best for: The Hive excels for post-hiking meals after Rocky Mountain National Park days. Families appreciate the kids' menu, casual atmosphere, and variety (wild game for adventurous eaters, familiar options for kids).

Groups of 4-8 handle the space well with no complaints about cramping. Beer enthusiasts get house-brewed craft options you can't find elsewhere. The pool tables downstairs create a casual hangout vibe for those wanting drinks without full meals.

Looking for other options? Check Estes Park restaurants with views, Antlers Rooftop Tavern, or Ember Restaurant for Lake Estes views.

Not ideal for: Fine dining seekers should look elsewhere ‚Äî this runs casual with no dress code expectations. Quick meals don't happen during dinner rush when waits hit 20-30 minutes without reservations.

Budget diners ordering higher-end entrees (ribeye $42, filet $45) will spend $50-100 per person with drinks. Vegetarians have options (caprese panini, pasta, pizza) but the menu clearly showcases wild game and meat.

The moderate noise level works for conversation but isn't quiet or romantic. Similar to Bird & Jim, this targets meat-eaters primarily.

Complete Practical Guide

Parking and arrival: Free parking lot at 470 Prospect Village Dr with plenty of spaces. No parking meters or validation needed. The entrance takes you directly upstairs to the restaurant.

If arriving during peak dinner (6-7:30pm), expect 20-30 minute waits without reservations. Multiple reviews mention the wait moves quickly, and you can grab drinks downstairs while waiting.

Reservations strategy: Call (970) 586-5421 for dinner reservations, especially Friday-Saturday. Walk-ins work for lunch and early dinner (before 6pm). One reviewer noted arriving at opening (11am) to avoid crowds worked well.

Getting there from Denver takes about 90 minutes ‚Äî see complete guide to getting to Estes Park.

Best times to visit: Weekday lunches (Wed-Mon 11am-2pm) stay less crowded than dinner service. Early dinner (5-6pm) or late dinner (8pm+) avoids peak rush. The restaurant closes Tuesday, so plan accordingly. After dining, you might explore nearby hot springs for post-meal relaxation.

Money-Saving Tips & Hacks

Lunch pricing mirrors dinner for most items, but arriving during off-peak hours (Wed-Thu lunch) means no wait and same menu at same prices with smaller crowds.

The caprese panini ($16) with side salad creates a satisfying meal under $25 total. Fish and chips ($18) provides generous portions that several reviewers mention taking leftovers home.

Strategic ordering for groups: Split one appetizer between 2-3 people (sausage sampler $18, fried green beans $12) rather than ordering multiple starters. The portions run large enough that reviews mention "enough to feed three people" from one sampler plus mac and cheese.

Kids menu items ($8-12) work for light adult appetites, saving $8-15 compared to full entrees. Free beer samples at the downstairs bar help you decide what to order without paying for test pints.

üí° Money Hack: Order the potato leek soup bowl ($10), side salad ($8), and split an appetizer ($12-18) between two people. Total cost: $30-36 for two people versus $40-60 if ordering two entrees. Multiple reviews praise the soup as "absolutely delicious" making this a quality budget option.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about The Hive

How much does dinner cost at The Hive?

Expect $20-30 per person for burgers/casual entrees with a beer or cocktail. Higher-end entrees (bison short ribs $36, rack of lamb $38, ribeye $42, filet $45) push you toward $50-65 per person with drinks and appetizers. Couples typically spend $60-100 total for dinner with shared appetizer and drinks.

Do I need reservations at The Hive?

Reservations help for dinner (especially Friday-Saturday) but aren't required. Multiple reviews mention 20-30 minute waits without reservations during peak dinner hours. Walk-ins work well for lunch and early/late dinner. Call (970) 586-5421 to reserve. One review noted "no wait" times, suggesting weekday lunches stay uncrowded.

Where do I park for The Hive?

Free parking lot on-site at 470 Prospect Village Dr. Multiple reviews mention "plenty of parking" with no issues finding spaces even during busy times. No parking meters, no validation needed, no paid lots required. Street parking exists as backup but unnecessary given the free lot.

What is The Hive known for?

House-brewed craft beer (raspberry wheat, gold lager, Lazarus most popular), elk burgers (166 review mentions), and bison short ribs (called "AMAZING" in multiple reviews). The 4.9-star rating across 7,376+ reviews reflects consistent quality. Known for generous portions, friendly service (servers frequently mentioned by name), and two-level space with restaurant upstairs, pool tables and bar downstairs.

Does The Hive have vegetarian options?

Yes, with caprese panini (~$16), buffalo pasta (~$22), pizza options, and salads clearly available. However, the menu heavily features wild game (elk, bison, buffalo) and meat-focused entrees. Vegetarians have choices but fewer than omnivores. No specific vegan items mentioned in reviews, though pasta and pizza might accommodate with modifications.

Is The Hive kid-friendly?

Very kid-friendly with dedicated kids menu (chicken fingers, fish and chips, burgers $8-12). Multiple reviews mention children dining comfortably, including one stating "kids loved the buffalo pasta" and another saying "awesome for kids they have kid menu's." Casual atmosphere means no dress code stress. One review notes a child enjoyed the fish and chips plate.

Is The Hive worth the price?

For $20-30 per person, yes. The 4.9-star rating across 7,376+ reviews shows consistent execution. Portions run generous (reviews mention splitting items, taking leftovers home). Higher-end entrees ($36-45) match quality expectations with reviewers calling bison short ribs and lamb "the best ever." House-brewed beer adds value you can't get elsewhere in Estes Park.

When is the best time to go to The Hive?

For smallest crowds: weekday lunch (Wed-Thu 11am-2pm) or early dinner (5pm). The 6-7:30pm dinner rush creates 20-30 minute waits. Late dinner (8pm+) works for shorter waits. Note they're closed Tuesday. One reviewer arrived at 11am opening to avoid crowds successfully. Summer weekends stay busiest after Rocky Mountain National Park closes for the day.

Can I try the beer before ordering at The Hive?

Yes, the downstairs bar offers complimentary beer samples. Multiple reviews mention "free beer samples so time passed quickly" during waits. You can taste small pours of their house-brewed beers before committing to a full pint with your meal. The bartenders pour tastings of raspberry wheat, gold lager, stouts, and seasonal options.

What should first-time visitors order at The Hive?

Start with a beer flight ($12) to sample their house brews, then order either the elk burger ($20) for a classic experience or bison short ribs ($36) for their most-praised entree. Add fried green beans ($12) as an appetizer if dining with 2+ people. This combo covers their craft beer strength, wild game specialty, and popular appetizer.
